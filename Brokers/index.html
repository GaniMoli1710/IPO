Pros & Cons of Angel One

Pros Cons
Angel One charges zero account opening charges as well as zero annual maintenance charges for only the first year Call and trade fee is charged at an extra Rs 20 per executed order.
Zero brokerage charges for equity delivery and remaining segments like intraday and F&O which is flat Rs. 20 per trade. User Interface is a bit complex as it is not for beginners.

Pros & Cons of Zerodha

Pros Cons
You don't pay any brokerage when you buy shares using cash-and-carry (CNC). Delivery trade has no leverage. Once bought, shares get delivered to your demat account and if sold, shares will get debited from your demat account. Doesn't provide stock tips, research reports or recommendations.
They offer services under a simple pricing model. They charge 0.03% or Rs 20 per executed order, whichever is lower, regardless of the trade size. The maximum brokerage paid by the customer is Rs 20 per trade. Lifetime free AMC demat account plans are not available.
Zerodha is a self-clearing broker. They don't charge clearing charges from customers An additional charge of Rs 50 per executed order for MIS/BO/CO positions which are not square off by the customer.
Set long-standing stop loss and target orders for your stock investments. Call & Trade is available at an additional fee of Rs 50 per executed order.
